03Role & Ownership

What I owned, and where I went hands-on

I was the frontend tech lead and also one of the core ICs on the project. I directly worked across the editor, the widget system, and the Node-side SSR publish flow, because most of the important decisions only made sense when you looked at the full loop end to end.

What I owned

  • End-to-end architecture across the editor, runtime, widget library, schema contract, and publish pipeline.
  • The core data model itself: how Project / Page / Widget / Layer are shaped, restored, and consumed by both the editor and runtime.
  • Widget APIs, versioning rules, and the CDN loading strategy for internal and external components.
  • Frontend engineering conventions, scaffolding, docs, and the day-to-day technical direction for a 3-5 engineer team.
  • Adoption work with product, ops, and business teams across 4 business lines.

What I built myself

  • The first version of the editor core: three-pane communication model, StageStore, Stage abstraction, workspace, and dynamic settings panel.
  • The shared widget contract and rendering path: WidgetClass, editor wrapper, runtime wrapper, and the onRender-based component protocol.
  • The Node-side SSR publish chain: jsdom bootstrapping, external widget preload, renderToString, template assembly, CDN output, and graceful fallback.
  • The declarative trigger model for click, exposure, redirect, share, custom events, and cross-widget updates.

I also worked closely with backend, design, and analytics teams. Storage and publish APIs had backend ownership, but I defined the contracts and still went deep into the implementation where the frontend and server concerns met.
